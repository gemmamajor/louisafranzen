About Me

Louisa Franzen has been working in the beauty industry for the past 18 years. After training at Bournemouth and Poole college for beauty therapy, hairdressing and theatrical make up, she went on to open her first salon in Weymouth in 1999 at the age of 19. Louisa has always specialised in make up and nail enhancements. After spending 5 and a half years building up a strong, successful business she sold it after being head hunted to become an educator in the industry. Louisa then travelled around Europe teaching before opening her own Centre of Excellence training school and Serenity Nail and Beauty Spa in Dorchester, Dorset. Louisa sold this business when she decided to start her family and is now a very proud mum to Lily and Rosie. She carried on working part time whilst concentrating on being a mum. In 2012 Louisa decided to widen her expertise by training to become a Micro Pigmentation specialist with the highly acclaimed Natural Enhancement academy in London - providers of training to the NHS, Harvey Nichols and Harrods. She is qualified to carry out permanent cosmetic enhancements which she has found to be incredibly rewarding. She continues to invest in further training, to always keep up with the changes and advances that the industry offers, to enable her to provide the most up to do date advanced procedures and techniques. Louisa has recently upgraded her equipment and machinery to the most superior available within the industry. Louisa takes great pleasure in her work, enhancing client's features naturally; giving people back their lost confidence.

FAQs

What is it?

Permanent makeup imitates perfectly applied cosmetics, with natural-looking results that can be as subtle or as dramatic as you want.

The effect is achieved by infusing hypo-allergenic specialist pigments into the dermal layer of your skin, where they remain all day, every day.

The results fade gradually over 2-3 years, with a “colour boost” recommended 12 months after the initial application – to maintain a fresh appearance.

Who will it benefit?

  • People who have undergone medical treatment which has resulted in hair loss or thinning
  • Ladies who don’t have the time to apply their make-up in the mornings, let alone freshen-up several times throughout the day
  • People with sparce, fair or poorly shaped eyebrows
  • People with unsteady hands or those with poor eyesight
  • People who suffer with allergies when using conventional make-up
  • People who have been disfigured or badly scarred
  • People whose lips appear unsymmetrical
  • People who swim and exercise regularly and want their makeup to stay perfectly in place
  • People with oily skin who are prone to "shedding" makeup quickly
  • People who holiday regularly and wantminimal effort to look good in hot climates

FAQ

Are permanent cosmetics a form of tattooing?

Permanent cosmetic enhancement – also known as semi permanent make-up and micro-pigmentation – is an advanced form of tattooing, however the pigment is not infused as deeply in the skin and therefore the enhancement will not have the permanency of a conventional tattoo. The pigments used replicate the colour choices available with conventional cosmetics enabling the achievement of subtle translucent results.

Is the procedure safe?

A code of practice is followed that ensures a safe treatment,working only with hypoallergenic pigments that meet European medical standards and performing an allergy test before the treatment commences. The highest quality sterile probes are used, which are disposed of immediately after the treatment is completed.
